Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest planet in the Solar System. It is a gas giant made mostly of hydrogen and helium and is best known for its spectacular ring system. Saturn has a thick atmosphere with powerful winds and storms, and its average temperature is about -178°C (-288°F), making it one of the coldest planets in the Solar System.

Saturn rotates on its axis in about 10.7 hours, making it one of the fastest-spinning planets. It takes about 29.5 Earth years to complete one orbit around the Sun. The planet has more than 140 known moons, including Titan, its largest moon, and Enceladus, which is famous for its icy geysers. Saturn's rings are made up of billions of pieces of ice, rock, and dust. They are the largest and most visible rings of any planet and stretch hundreds of thousands of kilometers into space.

Saturn is named after the Roman god of agriculture and wealth. Because of its beautiful rings and many moons, Saturn is one of the most recognizable planets in the night sky. Scientists continue to study Saturn and its moons to learn more about the formation and evolution of planets and planetary systems.
